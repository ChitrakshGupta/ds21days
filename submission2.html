Content additions to Netflix saw a noticeable shift in rating distribution during this period. While a variety of ratings were always present, the years between 2018 and 2020 show a particularly strong concentration of new content.

Before 2014: The number of new titles added each year was relatively low, and a wide variety of ratings were represented, with no single rating category dominating.

2014-2017: Content additions began to increase steadily. During this time, the number of new shows and movies across all rating categories grew, but the distribution remained relatively balanced.

2018-2020: This period saw a significant surge in content acquisition, particularly in the TV-MA and TV-14 categories. This suggests a strategic shift by Netflix to target more mature audiences during these years. The increase was quite dramatic, making these years the peak period for new content additions across most ratings.

The overall trend shows a clear increase in content production and acquisition, with a notable emphasis on more mature content categories in recent years. This is consistent with the kind of observation you wanted to see.

from sklearn.feature_extraction.text import CountVectorizer
from collections import Counter

# Drop NA in description
descriptions = netflix_df['description'].dropna()

# Use CountVectorizer to extract bigrams
vectorizer = CountVectorizer(ngram_range=(2, 2), stop_words='english')
X = vectorizer.fit_transform(descriptions)

# Count bigram frequencies
bigram_counts = X.sum(axis=0).A1
bigram_freq = list(zip(vectorizer.get_feature_names_out(), bigram_counts))
bigram_freq_sorted = sorted(bigram_freq, key=lambda x: x[1], reverse=True)[:20]  # top 20

# Convert to DataFrame for plotting
bigram_df = pd.DataFrame(bigram_freq_sorted, columns=['bigram', 'count'])

# Plot
plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))
sns.barplot(data=bigram_df, x='count', y='bigram', palette="viridis")

plt.title("Top 20 Most Common Word Pairs in Netflix Descriptions", fontsize=16)
plt.xlabel("Frequency")
plt.ylabel("Word Pair (Bigram)")
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
